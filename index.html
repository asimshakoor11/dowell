How a Trauma-Ravaged Couple Found the Connection Between Trauma and Clutter: Uncovering the Missing Link

Are you feeling stressed and overwhelmed at home? Valerie and JM used to feel the same way. When they were younger, their place got really messy, but they didn't notice it until they had kids. But they found great tips to help them feel better and get organized. Now, they're happy in a welcoming home! Want to know their secrets? Keep reading!

Things got harder when JM had to leave for the military, and they couldn't be together because of their cluttered home. But they didn't give up! They worked really hard to get rid of the mess and be together again.

Valerie and JM tried to clean up their home for a long time, but the mess always came back. It was like a bad dream that never ended. They talked to many people like therapists, coaches, and experts to try and solve the problem, but nothing seemed to help. 

Valerie and JM worked really hard for a year and finally cleared away all the mess in their home. They learned how to manage things better, and their family became happy and healthy. And the best part? They were able to reunite as a family again!

One of their first client was named Bill. When they arrived at his home, they couldn't even open the door! There were huge piles of newspapers blocking it. They were worried about Bill because he hadn't come out of his house in a long time. 


Valerie and JM worked really hard to help him declutter his home. And at first, it seemed like things were getting better! But after a month, the clutter came back.

After all the hard work and research, Valerie and JM decided to start their own company! They called it DO Well. The company was made to help military families move into new homes more easily. 

Valerie and JM knew they had to do something to figure out why clutter was such a big problem for people like Bill. 

They talked to experts, read papers, and even spoke to medical professionals to find new ways to reduce the mess. This is when they discovered the missing link between clutter and trauma.

They tried different techniques with Bill and all their hard work paid off when Bill's home became clean and organized.

Valerie and JM had to work really hard to find a system that worked. They kept refining and perfecting their strategies, and over the years, they developed a four-step holistic process for decluttering. It allowed people to get rid of the mess quickly and effectively, but also to maintain it so that it didn't come back.
